# Caddyshack App Instructions

## Product naming

- Use `Matchday` across the app, not `Gameweek`.
- The main dashboard experience is the `Matchday` page.
- Odds should always be shown in decimal format.

## Betting data

- Ladbrokes is the bookmaker of record for suggested matchday options.
- When generating or updating options, compare against Ladbrokes real-world odds or the latest local Ladbrokes snapshot data.
- If live Ladbrokes odds are not available in-app, use the structured local snapshot as the source of truth until refreshed.
- When assessing whether a leg is suitable, do not rely on odds and form alone.
- Also consider the latest credible context that could positively or negatively affect expected team performance, including:
  - team news
  - club news
  - manager news
  - injuries and suspensions
  - likely absences or rotation risk
  - tactical changes or role changes
  - fixture congestion, travel, or recovery concerns
  - any other credible signal that materially changes the expected game state

## Matchday option structure

- Each matchday should present three risk groups:
  - Defensive
  - Balanced
  - Aggressive
- Each risk group is an acca option made up of match segments.
- Match segments within an acca must always be displayed in chronological order by match date and kickoff time.
- Each segment should show:
  - market label
  - decimal odds
  - a very small strapline for day and kickoff time
- The AI-recommended risk group should be chosen from both market fit and bankroll context, not from odds alone.
- Recommendation logic should consider:
  - current pot relative to the initial deposit baseline
  - recent settled matchday outcomes and bankroll direction
  - whether recent betting results are strong, mixed, or deteriorating
- If recent results are strong and the pot is healthy, the recommendation can lean more aggressive when justified by the slate.
- If recent results are mixed or the pot is roughly stable, the recommendation should usually stay balanced unless the market clearly argues otherwise.
- If the pot is dwindling or recent results are poor, the recommendation should become more protective.
- Match selection and recommendation logic should also account for whether the latest team, club, or manager news materially strengthens or weakens confidence in any proposed leg.

## Staking rules

- Stake values must be derived from the current pot rather than hardcoded.
- The original member deposits form the initial pot baseline.
- Stake ambition should consider:
  - current pot relative to the initial pot
  - how many matchdays have already completed
- Aggressive stake is the anchor stake and should sit near half the pot, flexing with bankroll growth and season progress.
- Defensive and Balanced stakes should be derived relative to the Aggressive stake using each option's total decimal odds.

## Cashout rules

- Every risk group should include a cashout guidance block.
- Cashout guidance must include:
  - lower cashout target
  - upper cashout target
  - no cashout value
  - what to watch on the day
- Lower and upper cashout values are strategy guidance.
- No cashout value is informational only.
- Cashout guidance should reflect the risk profile:
  - Defensive protects cashout value earlier
  - Balanced balances protection and upside
  - Aggressive prioritises upside over early protection
- Cashout guidance should be specific to the actual proposed legs in that strategy, not generic boilerplate.
- When generating cashout advice, check the latest credible team news for the clubs used in the proposed legs, including:
  - broader club news and manager news where relevant
  - injuries
  - suspensions
  - likely absences or rotation risk
  - any notable attacking or defensive availability changes
- The `what to watch on the day` advice should mention the main in-play patterns that matter for that exact strategy, for example:
  - whether an early goal helps or hurts the slip structure
  - whether a slow start makes the cashout less attractive
  - whether missing forwards, defenders, or goalkeepers change the expected game state
  - whether the strategy depends more on control, clean-sheet protection, or open-game goal trading
- The `what to watch on the day` advice should also call out match-specific events that could change cashout value quickly, for example:
  - a likely upset developing in a shorter-priced leg
  - a key striker, creator, centre-back, or goalkeeper being substituted
  - a tactical switch that weakens the original bet angle
  - a visible loss of control, territory, or chance quality in a favourite
  - game-state changes that especially damage a goals-based leg, such as a defensive shutdown or an early attacking injury
- Defensive cashout advice should focus on protecting value and warning signs that justify an earlier exit.
- Balanced cashout advice should focus on when to protect a solid return versus when the remaining edge still supports staying in.
- Aggressive cashout advice should focus on upside, but still identify the key negative team-news or in-play patterns that would justify taking value rather than letting it ride.
- When structured proposal data supports it, store these watchouts as proposal-level cashout watch items so the app can render match-specific advice instead of generic fallback text.

## Selection and persistence

- Do not preselect a risk group for the logged-in user by default.
- The AI recommendation badge can be shown without creating a selected state.
- The logged-in user's selected risk group should be simulated as persisted with local storage.
- On load, restore the logged-in user's local persisted choice if present.

## Custom bet rules

- The app can also support one-off custom bets outside the matchday voting flow.
- Custom bets are single recommendations with no voting and no matchday card.
- A custom bet should usually present a ranked shortlist of `2` or `3` proposed bets for the event, ordered from most recommended to least recommended.
- Custom bets should be used for event-led opportunities such as horse racing, football specials, or golf tournament markets.
- When generating a custom bet, the AI should:
  - create or replace one local custom-bet row for the requested event window
  - monitor the requested sport and event market
  - check the current Ladbrokes odds and market shape for the event
  - review the latest relevant media and event context
  - look for circumstances, news, tactics, or event-specific angles that could positively or negatively influence the betting outcome
  - present `2` or `3` proposed bets that fit the betting format requested by the user
  - order those bets from strongest overall recommendation to weakest
- Custom bet ids must not be just the event name.
- Prefix custom bet ids and slugs with the event date so they stay unique, for example:
  - `custom-bet-2026-04-11-grand-national-each-way`
  - `2026-04-11-grand-national-each-way`
- For horse-racing custom bets, the AI should also:
  - always download the jockey silks / jersey image for each proposed horse into `public/assets/jockey_jerseys/` when a credible source is available
  - store the local silks image path in the horse-racing proposed-bet detail block when available
  - gather richer horse profile detail beyond odds alone, including age, weight, official rating, recent form, and any other credible race factors typically considered
  - consider horse-specific signals such as owner, stable confidence, prep run quality, stamina evidence, jumping reliability, going suitability, distance suitability, and expected pace shape when relevant
- For football custom bets, fetch club badge assets into the existing local club assets when the recommendation depends on clubs that are not already covered by the app.
- For golf custom bets, fetch an event or player image asset only when there is a clear credible source and the app has a place to use it; otherwise prefer no extra asset over a weak one.
- Newly generated custom bets should start in a `pending` state until an admin records the real placed bet details.
- Custom bets should still present:
  - the proposed bets
  - the odds for each proposed bet
  - a suggested stake amount based on the current pot
  - the key event dates and times
  - cashout advice in the same spirit as matchday guidance
- The top-ranked proposed bet should still be stored as the primary recommendation for timeline summaries and admin placement flows.
- The AI Analysis block for a custom bet should cover the shortlist together, explaining:
  - why the bets were ranked in that order
  - how the options provide different coverage or tradeoffs
  - which event, market, and news factors were considered across the shortlist
- Each proposed bet should still carry a short per-bet summary that can sit directly under the bet row in the UI.
- Suggested custom-bet stake sizing should be derived from the current pot, not hardcoded.
- For custom bets, the AI should recommend a stake that reflects:
  - the current pot size
  - the volatility of the event
  - whether the format is win-only, each-way, or another lower-coverage / higher-coverage structure
  - the fact that a one-off bet should usually risk less of the pot than the main aggressive matchday stake
- The suggested stake amount should be stored separately from the actual placed stake.
- The actual placed stake remains empty until an admin records the real bet placement.
- Custom-bet cashout guidance should include:
  - lower cashout target
  - upper cashout target
  - no cashout value
  - specific event-related watchouts that matter for the live position
- Custom-bet cashout watchouts should be specific to the sport and event, for example:
  - horse racing: market drift, going changes, paddock signals, jockey changes, horse fitness signs, jumping rhythm, or pace-shape clues
  - football: substitutions, tactical switches, red cards, injuries, and visible control or momentum swings
  - golf: weather shifts, player fitness or swing issues, round momentum, and leaderboard pressure
- The custom bet should appear on the timeline as a `Custom Bet`.
- Clicking a custom bet timeline entry should take the user to the dedicated custom bet page, not the matchday page.
- Custom bet data should be stored in dedicated custom-bet data files and corresponding database tables rather than mixed into matchday rows.
- Horse-racing custom bet detail should include as much credible structured horse information as is available, such as:
  - racecourse
  - race time
  - horse name
  - trainer
  - jockey
  - jockey silks image path
  - silks source url when known
  - age
  - weight
  - official rating
  - recent form
  - owner when known
  - going
  - distance
  - field size
- The recommended horse should always include the core racecard-style summary fields when credible:
  - official rating
  - weight
  - age
  - recent form in recognised racing notation such as `1421`
- Placement-specific values such as actual stake used, actual placed odds, and placed date/time should stay empty until an admin marks the custom bet as placed.
- The AI-generated suggested stake amount should stay available even before the admin records the real placed stake.
- Future custom bets can be cleared by local or remote future-clear scripts.
- Staked custom bets must not be removed by future-clear scripts unless a force flag is explicitly used.
- Past custom bets must never be deleted by future-clear scripts.

## Ledger and pot rules

- Any ledger entry that increases the pot should be treated as positive and shown with a green up-right arrow.
- Any ledger entry that removes money from the pot should be treated as negative and shown with a red down-right arrow.
- A stake placement is a negative ledger entry.
